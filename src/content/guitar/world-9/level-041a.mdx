# Level 41: Basic Fingerpicking Patterns

## Lesson

**Fingerpicking** is the art of plucking individual strings with your fingers rather than using a pick. This technique allows you to play melody, harmony, and bass lines simultaneously, creating a full, orchestral sound. The basic fingerpicking pattern uses your thumb (P) for bass notes on the lower strings and your index (i), middle (m), and ring (a) fingers for melody on the higher strings. Start with simple alternating bass patterns where your thumb alternates between two bass strings while your fingers play a steady pattern on the treble strings. This creates a "boom-chick" rhythm that's the foundation of folk, country, and classical guitar. The key is keeping your wrist relaxed and your fingers close to the strings for precise control.

## Exercise

Learn basic fingerpicking with this step-by-step approach:
1. Position your thumb on the 5th string (A string) and your index finger on the 2nd string (B string)
2. Play the 5th string with your thumb, then the 2nd string with your index finger
3. Now play the 4th string (D string) with your thumb, then the 3rd string (G string) with your middle finger
4. The pattern is: thumb (5th), index (2nd), thumb (4th), middle (3rd)
5. Practice this slowly until it feels natural and even
6. Add your ring finger: thumb (5th), index (1st), middle (2nd), ring (3rd)
7. Practice the full pattern: P-i-m-a on strings 5-1-2-3
8. Apply this pattern to C Major chord, keeping the bass notes steady
9. Focus on making each note clear and distinct
10. Gradually increase speed while maintaining even timing

Fingerpicking creates beautiful, full arrangements!
