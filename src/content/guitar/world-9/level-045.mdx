# Level 45: Classical Tremolo

## Lesson

Tremolo is the rapid repetition of a single note or alternating between two notes, creating a vibrating, shimmering effect. In classical guitar, the standard tremolo pattern uses the thumb (p), index (i), and middle (m) fingers in sequence: p-i-m-i or p-i-a-i. This technique allows a single note to sound like a continuous stream of notes, adding incredible expressiveness to your playing. Tremolo is essential for classical pieces like "Recuerdos de la Alhambra" and many flamenco compositions. The key is consistent finger alternation and maintaining even volume across all notes. Practice with a metronome starting very slowly to build proper muscle memory.
