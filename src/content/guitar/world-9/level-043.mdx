# Level 43: Travis Picking Advanced

## Lesson

Advanced Travis picking incorporates chord changes within the pattern, requiring your left hand to switch shapes while your right hand maintains the alternating thumb pattern. This technique allows you to create full arrangements that include harmonic movement while keeping the signature syncopated feel. The challenge lies in coordinating two independent motor tasks—fretting chords while picking patterns. Start with simple two-chord progressions before attempting complex changes. Listen to Merle Travis, Chet Atkins, and Tommy Emmanuel to understand the musical possibilities. The goal is to make each note ring clearly while maintaining the driving thumb pattern underneath.

## Exercise

Master advanced Travis picking with chord transitions:
1. Start with the basic Travis pattern in open position (no left hand chords)
2. Add a simple G chord with your left hand while continuing the pattern
3. Practice playing four beats of Travis pattern on G chord
4. Now transition to C chord—keep thumb pattern steady during the switch
5. Practice switching between G and C in time, holding each for four beats
6. Add D chord to the progression: G to C to D and back to G
7. Focus on making each note ring clearly, especially during chord changes
8. Practice the full progression at 70 BPM, then gradually increase to 90 BPM
9. Listen to each chord change—any muddiness means you need more practice
10. Record yourself and compare to professional examples to check timing
