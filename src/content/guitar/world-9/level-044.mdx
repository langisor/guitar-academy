# Level 44: Travis Picking Master

## Lesson

Master-level Travis picking involves playing three-note patterns where your thumb hits three different bass notes per measure. This creates more rhythmic complexity and allows for elaborate arrangements that fill the musical space. The three-note pattern typically includes root, fifth, and octave bass notes cycling through. This technique is signature to players like Chet Atkins and Tommy Emmanuel who have perfected this style over decades. Practice this level only after you have mastered the basic and advanced patterns with ease. Focus on musicality and dynamics rather than raw speed—true mastery shows in the sound, not just the speed.

## Exercise

Achieve mastery with the three-note Travis pattern:
1. Review your two-note Travis pattern until it feels completely automatic
2. Introduce a third thumb strike: play 5th string, then 4th string, then 6th string (root-octave-fifth pattern)
3. Count carefully: thumb on beat 1, thumb on beat 2, fingers on beat 2-and, thumb on beat 3
4. The pattern is: 5th string (down), 4th string (down), fingers (up), 6th string (down)
5. Practice this pattern slowly until it feels natural at 60 BPM
6. Add chord shapes: play G shape while maintaining the three-note thumb pattern
7. Focus on the 6th string (low E) bass note—make it ring clearly
8. Practice transitioning between chords while keeping the three-note pattern
9. Try the progression G to D to C, maintaining the pattern throughout
10. Work up to 100 BPM while maintaining perfect timing and clarity
