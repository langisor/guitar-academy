# Level 41: Travis Picking

## Lesson

The fingerpicking style popularized by Merle Travis revolutionized acoustic guitar playing and influenced countless musicians. In Travis picking, your thumb alternates between the bass notes on the lower strings while your fingers pick melody notes on the higher strings. This creates a full, orchestral sound where a single guitarist can sound like a full band. The key is maintaining a steady thumb pulse while your fingers play independent melodic patterns. Start slowly and focus on keeping your thumb movement consistent—speed will come with practice. Once mastered, Travis picking opens doors to countless folk, country, and blues arrangements.

## Exercise

Practice the Travis picking pattern with these steps:
1. Position your thumb on the 5th string (A string) and your index finger on the 2nd string (B string)
2. Play the 5th string with your thumb, then immediately pluck the 2nd string with your index finger
3. Next, play the 4th string (D string) with your thumb, then the 3rd string (G string) with your middle finger
4. Continue alternating: thumb on 5th, index on 2nd, thumb on 4th, middle on 3rd
5. Keep your wrist relaxed and your thumb striking downward in a comfortable arc
6. Maintain a slow tempo of 60 BPM initially—accuracy is more important than speed
7. Practice each transition slowly until the movement feels natural
8. Gradually increase tempo to 80 BPM while maintaining consistent thumb rhythm
9. Add the G chord shape with your left hand while maintaining the picking pattern
10. Practice until you can play the pattern smoothly without thinking
