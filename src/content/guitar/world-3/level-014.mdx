# Level 14: Em → Am

## Lesson

The **E Minor to A Minor transition** is a great example of chord relationships. **Am is just Em moved down one string set** with an added index finger. In Em, your fingers are on the 2nd fret of the G and A strings. To form Am, move that same shape down one string (to the G and D strings) and add your index finger on the 1st fret of the B string. This is essentially the same finger shape, just shifted. This transition helps you understand how chords are related and how barre chords eventually work. Practice feeling the "family resemblance" between these two minor chords.
