# Level 11: G → C

## Lesson

Transitioning between chords smoothly is essential for playing songs. The **G to C transition** uses your **middle finger as a pivot**. In the G chord, your middle finger is on the 2nd fret of the A string. When moving to C, that same finger goes to the 2nd fret of the D string—almost the same position. Keep your fingers in their shapes and move them together as a unit. The key is to keep your fingers close to the fretboard and minimize unnecessary movement. Practice this transition slowly at first, then gradually increase speed as the movement becomes muscle memory.
