# Level 13: D → Em

## Lesson

The **D to E Minor transition** is one of the easiest chord changes because you simply **remove your middle and ring fingers** from the D chord to form Em. In D Major, your index finger is on the 2nd fret of the G string, middle on the 3rd fret of the B string, and ring on the 2nd fret of the D string. For Em, lift your middle and ring fingers while keeping your index finger down on the G string—it's already in the right position! This transition teaches you to minimize unnecessary finger movement. Master this, and you'll understand how efficient chord changes work.

## Exercise

Transition between D and Em 10 times:
1. Start with your D Major chord shape
2. Lift your middle and ring fingers off the fretboard
3. Keep your index finger pressed on the 2nd fret of the G string
4. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the A string
5. Check that all notes ring clearly
6. Return to D Major and repeat 10 times
7. Focus on minimal finger movement

The key is keeping your index finger anchored.
