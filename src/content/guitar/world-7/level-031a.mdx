# Level 31: Vibrato Technique

## Lesson

**Vibrato** is the subtle, controlled variation in pitch that adds expression, emotion, and sustain to your notes. It's what makes a note "sing" rather than just sound. Vibrato is created by rocking your fretting finger back and forth (or side to side) while maintaining pressure on the string. There are two main types: **hand vibrato** (rocking the entire hand from the wrist) and **finger vibrato** (bending the finger joint). The key is consistency—the oscillation should be even and controlled, not erratic. Vibrato is essential for lead guitar, blues, and rock playing. It's what separates mechanical playing from musical expression. Start with slow, wide vibrato and gradually develop speed and subtlety.

## Exercise

Develop your vibrato technique with this systematic approach:
1. Place your ring finger on the 7th fret of the G string
2. Press firmly and rock your hand back and forth from the wrist
3. The note should bend slightly sharp and then return to pitch
4. Focus on even, consistent oscillation—like a sine wave
5. Practice slow, wide vibrato first: bend about a quarter-tone sharp and back
6. Count: 1-2-3-4 for each oscillation cycle
7. Gradually increase speed while maintaining control
8. Try vibrato with different fingers (index, middle, ring, pinky)
9. Practice vibrato on sustained notes in scales and melodies
10. Listen to guitarists with great vibrato (B.B. King, David Gilmour) and imitate

Vibrato is your voice on the guitar—develop it with patience!
