# Level 33: Interval Shapes

## Lesson

**Intervals** are the distances between notes, and understanding them is essential for advanced guitar playing. **Octaves** and **power chords** (roots and 5ths) are the most useful interval shapes for rhythm guitar. An octave is the same note at a higher pitch—playing a note on one string and again 12 frets higher, or two strings apart (like low E and D). Power chords are simply the root note plus the perfect fifth, played without the third. They're essential in rock and heavy music because they sound powerful and don't muddy the mix. The technique is simple: **skip the middle string** and play root on one string plus the same note on the string two positions higher.

## Exercise

Practice octave shapes:
1. Find any note on the low E string
2. Find the same note on the D string (two strings apart)
3. That's an octave—play both notes together
4. Now find the 5th interval (7 frets up from root)
5. Play root plus 5th together (power chord)
6. Practice this on every string position
7. Focus on skipping the middle string (E-A, A-D, D-G, B-e)
8. Practice the root-5th pattern up and down the neck

These shapes are essential for rock rhythm playing!
