# Level 34: Triads

## Lesson

**Triads** are three-note chords built from the 1st, 3rd, and 5th degrees of a scale. They're compact, efficient shapes that you can play on just three strings. There are four main types: **Major** (1-3-5), **Minor** (1-b3-5), **Diminished** (1-b3-b5), and **Augmented** (1-3-#5). Each type has a distinct sound: major is bright, minor is sad, diminished is tense, and augmented is dreamy. Triads are incredibly useful because they let you play full chords in small, movable shapes across the neck. Learning them on strings 1-2-3 (top three strings) gives you flexibility for comping and soloing.

## Exercise

Practice all triad shapes:
1. Start with the A string root position
2. Play C-E-G (C Major triad) on strings 2-1-3
3. Play C-Eb-G (C Minor triad) one fret lower
4. Play C-Eb-Gb (C Diminished) another fret lower
5. Play C-E-G# (C Augmented) starting from C Major
6. Practice moving these shapes up the neck
7. Practice on strings 1-2-3, then 2-3-4
8. Practice moving between major and minor

Compact shapes—maximum flexibility!
