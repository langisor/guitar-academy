# Level 31: Note Names

## Lesson

The **chromatic scale** contains all 12 notes used in Western music: **C, C#, D, D#, E, F, F#, G, G#, A, A#, B**. There are 12 semitones (half-steps) between each repetition of the same note. Two important natural half-steps exist where there are no sharps or flats: **B to C** and **E to F**. These are the only places where consecutive letters don't have a sharp/flat between them. Every other note relationship includes a sharp or flat. Understanding the chromatic scale is fundamental to navigating the fretboard, understanding scales, and learning music theory.
