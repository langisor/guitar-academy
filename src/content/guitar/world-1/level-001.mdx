# Level 1: Guitar Parts

## Goal
Learn the essential parts of your guitar and their functions.

## Lesson

### The Headstock
The headstock is at the top of the guitar and contains the tuning pegs. These pegs allow you to tighten or loosen the strings to tune your guitar.

### The Tuning Pegs
Most guitars have 6 tuning pegs - 3 on each side of the headstock. Turning them adjusts the tension of the strings.

### The Nut
The nut is a small strip near the headstock where the strings pass through. It keeps the strings spaced evenly and at the correct height.

### The Frets
The metal strips running down the neck are called frets. Pressing a string against a fret changes the pitch of the note.

### The Fretboard
The fretboard (or fingerboard) is the flat surface beneath the strings where you press down to play notes and chords.

### The Soundhole (Acoustic)
On acoustic guitars, the soundhole is where the sound projects from. On electric guitars, there are pickups instead.

### The Bridge
The bridge holds the strings in place at the bottom of the guitar and affects the action (string height).

## Exercise
Point to each part of your guitar as you identify it:
1. Headstock
2. Tuning Pegs
3. Nut
4. Frets
5. Fretboard
6. Soundhole/Pickups
7. Bridge

## Challenge
Can you name all 7 parts without looking? Practice until you can!
