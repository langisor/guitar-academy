# Level 3: Tuning Basics

## Lesson

**Standard tuning** from lowest to highest string is **E-A-D-G-B-e**. We use tuners to measure "pitch"—the frequency of a vibrating string. If a note is too high, it's "sharp" and you need to loosen the string. If too low, it's "flat" and you need to tighten it. Electronic tuners make this easy by showing a needle or display. When the needle is centered (or the light turns green), the string is in tune. Always tune before playing, as strings can stretch and go out of tune during practice. This standard tuning works for most music and allows you to play chords and scales across the entire fretboard.
