# Level 2: Proper Posture

## Lesson

Sit with good **posture** and hold the guitar close to your body. Rest it on your right leg (or left if you're left-handed). Keep your back straight and shoulders relaxed. The guitar neck should angle slightly upward, making it easier to reach the frets. Your dominant hand (strumming hand) should rest over the body of the guitar near the soundhole, while your fretting hand forms chords near the nut. Good posture prevents fatigue and makes playing more comfortable. Avoid slouching or holding the guitar too far from your body—these habits lead to tension and pain during practice.
