# Level 36: Smoke on the Water

## Lesson

**"Smoke on the Water"** by Deep Purple features one of the most recognizable guitar riffs in rock history. The famous opening, meant to mimic the sound of a casino burning down, uses a simple **power chord pattern**: G5 - A5 - D5. The rhythm is distinctive: down-down-down-pause, then repeat two more times with the power chords. The entire riff uses just two notes per chord—root and fifth. This song proves that iconic rock music can be built on simple foundations. The key is capturing that driving, heavy rhythm that defined early hard rock.

## Exercise

Learn the iconic riff:
1. Start with the G5 power chord (3rd fret, low E and A strings together)
2. Play three down strums on G5
3. Skip one beat, then play A5 (5th fret, A and D strings)
4. Play three down strums on A5
5. Skip one beat, then play D5 (5th fret, D and G strings)
6. Play three down strums on D5
7. Repeat the entire sequence until it flows
8. Focus on the driving rhythm and heavy feel

Power through it—it's simpler than it sounds!
