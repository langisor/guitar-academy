# Level 37: Seven Nation Army

## Lesson

**"Seven Nation Army"** by The White Stripes features one of the simplest yet most powerful riffs in modern rock. The entire melody is played on just **one note**—the G at the 3rd fret of the low E string—creating a memorable hook that transcended the song itself. The riff moves from the 3rd fret (G) to the open high e string, creating that distinctive low-to-high sweep. The song shows that you don't need complex chords or advanced techniques to create an iconic rock sound. One note, played with the right rhythm, can define a generation.

## Exercise

Learn the riff:
1. Start at the 3rd fret of the low E string (G note)
2. Play four times: 3-3-3-3 (four down strums at the same fret)
3. Then play the open high e string (0)
4. Then play the 3rd fret of the high e string (3)
5. The pattern is: 3-3-3-3-0-3
6. Practice slowly until you can play it cleanly
7. Then increase to the song tempo of 116 BPM
8. Add a slight palm mute for the "bite" sound

One note can be a hit—prove it with this riff!
