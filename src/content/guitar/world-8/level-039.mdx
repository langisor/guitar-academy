# Level 39: Back in Black

## Lesson

**"Back in Black"** by AC/DC features one of the most driving, aggressive riffs in rock history. The opening riff uses an **A power chord** with heavy **palm muting** to create that tight, percussive rhythm that defines hard rock. The riff is simple—root note on the 5th fret of the A string with the D and G strings—played with aggressive down strums and palm muting. The key is the "chugga-chugga" rhythm created by damping the strings between strums. This technique, called palm muting, adds tightness and definition to your rhythm playing. Combined with the band's signature overdrive tone, it's one of the most recognizable rock sounds ever.
