# Level 38: Iron Man

## Lesson

**"Iron Man"** by Black Sabbath features one of the most menacing and recognizable guitar riffs in heavy metal history. The opening riff uses a distinctive **E power chord** pattern with a heavy, slow, palm-muted feel. The riff is built around the open low E string, moving to the 2nd fret (F#), creating tension that resolves back down. The slow, deliberate rhythm and the "wah" effect create an ominous atmosphere that perfectly matches the song's themes. This riff teaches you the power of simplicity combined with the right tone and feel—slow and heavy can be more impactful than fast and flashy.

## Exercise

Master the opening riff:
1. Start with the open low E string (muted or lightly palm-muted)
2. Play the 2nd fret of the low E string (F#)
3. Play the 2nd fret again
4. Return to open low E
5. Play the 2nd fret again
6. Return to open low E
7. The pattern is: open-2-2-open-2-open
8. Play slowly at 104 BPM with heavy palm muting
9. Feel the weight and intensity in each note

Slow and heavy—the Iron Man way!
