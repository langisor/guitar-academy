# Level 49: Performance Mindset

## Lesson

Performing live requires more than technical skill—it demands mental preparation, stage presence, and the ability to connect with your audience. Before any performance, visualize success and mentally walk through your setlist, anticipating any challenging sections. Stage presence involves standing confidently, making eye contact, and conveying the emotion of your music through your body language. Connection with your audience starts with acknowledging them and creating moments of shared experience through your playing. Preparation is your safety net—the more you've practiced, the more you can relax and improvise. Remember that audiences want you to succeed; they're rooting for you. Focus on the joy of sharing music rather than fear of mistakes.

## Exercise

Build your performance mindset with these preparation steps:
1. Create a realistic setlist that challenges you but doesn't overwhelm you
2. Practice your full set at least three times in complete runs
3. Record yourself performing the full set and watch it back critically
4. Practice in front of a friend or family member weekly
5. Visualize your performance the night before: imagine walking on stage feeling confident
6. Prepare your guitar, stool, and any needed accessories before the gig
7. Develop a pre-performance routine (stretching, breathing, or playing through scales)
8. Plan your transitions between songs to minimize dead air
9. Prepare for something to go wrong—know how you'll handle a string break or memory lapse
10. On performance day, do a short practice session, then rest your hands before the show
