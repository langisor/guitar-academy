# Level 46: Syncopated Rhythms

## Lesson

Syncopation is the heart of groove—the deliberate placement of accents on off-beats that makes music feel alive and moving. When you strum on the "and" counts between the main beats, you create a sense of anticipation and drive that keeps the listener engaged. Rock, funk, and blues all rely heavily on syncopated strumming patterns to create their signature feels. The key is internalizing the pulse while accenting the unexpected moments. Practice feeling the beat as a steady heartbeat while your strumming hand dances around it. Start slowly and feel how the off-beat accents create tension that resolves on the downbeats.
