# Level 6: E Major

## Lesson

The **E Major chord** is a bright, full-sounding chord that uses all six strings. Place your **index finger** on the 1st fret of the G string, your **middle finger** on the 2nd fret of the A string, and your **ring finger** on the 2nd fret of the D string. All six strings are strummed, making this one of the fullest-sounding open chords. E Major is fundamental to rock, blues, and country music. The chord shape is easy to learn and serves as the foundation for the E-shape barre chords you'll learn later. This chord is often used as a "home base" in many songs and is essential for understanding chord progressions in the key of E.

## Exercise

Practice the E Major chord for 1 minute:
1. Place your index finger on the 1st fret of the G string
2. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the A string
3. Place your ring finger on the 2nd fret of the D string
4. Strum all six strings
5. Ensure every string rings clearly
6. Check that no fingers are muting adjacent strings
7. Practice transitioning to A Major
8. Repeat the process

This chord uses all six strings—make them all sing!
