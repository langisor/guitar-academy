# Level 6: A Minor

## Lesson

The **A Minor chord** is one of the easiest chords to learn and is essential for countless songs. It uses just two fingers: place your **index finger** on the 1st fret of the B string and your **middle finger** on the 2nd fret of the D string. The A, G, and high e strings are played open, while the low E string should not be strummed. A Minor has a melancholic, emotional sound that appears in everything from classical music to rock ballads. The chord shape is simple and serves as a foundation for learning more complex minor chords later. This is often the first minor chord beginners master.

## Exercise

Practice the A Minor chord for 1 minute:
1. Place your index finger on the 1st fret of the B string
2. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the D string
3. Strum from the A string down (skip the low E string)
4. Ensure all five strings ring clearly
5. Check that the open G string is not muted
6. Practice transitioning to and from C Major
7. Repeat the process

This is the easiest minor chord—master it first!
