# Level 8: D Major

## Lesson

The **D Major chord** forms a distinctive "triangle" shape on the fretboard. It's played with three fingers on the middle four strings while the two thinnest and two thickest strings remain open. Place your **index finger** on the 2nd fret of the G string, your **middle finger** on the 3rd fret of the B string, and your **ring finger** on the 2nd fret of the D string. This creates a compact, efficient chord shape. When strumming D Major, only play the top four strings (D, G, B, high e)—strumming the low E or A string will sound muddy or wrong. The D chord is essential in countless songs and is one of the first chords beginners master.
