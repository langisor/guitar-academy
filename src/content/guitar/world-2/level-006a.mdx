# Level 6: A Major

## Lesson

The **A Major chord** is a bright, open-sounding chord that uses three fingers clustered together on the 2nd fret. Place your **index finger** on the 2nd fret of the D string, your **middle finger** on the 2nd fret of the G string, and your **ring finger** on the 2nd fret of the B string. The low E string should not be played—strum from the A string down. All three fingers should be arched to avoid muting adjacent strings. A Major is one of the most common chords in music, appearing in countless songs across every genre. The tight finger grouping takes practice, but once mastered, it becomes second nature.

## Exercise

Practice the A Major chord for 1 minute:
1. Place your index finger on the 2nd fret of the D string
2. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the G string
3. Place your ring finger on the 2nd fret of the B string
4. Strum from the A string down (skip the low E string)
5. Ensure all three notes ring clearly
6. Check that no strings are muted by flat fingers
7. Repeat the process

Keep your fingers arched and close together!
