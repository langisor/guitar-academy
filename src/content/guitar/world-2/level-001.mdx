# Level 6: G Major Chord

## Goal
Learn to play the G Major chord cleanly.

## Lesson

### About G Major
G Major is one of the most common chords in guitar music. It's used in countless songs from folk to rock to pop.

### Finger Placement
The G chord requires 3 fingers:

| Finger | String | Fret |
|--------|--------|------|
| Index | Low E (6) | 3rd |
| Middle | A (5) | 2nd |
| Ring | D (4) | 3rd |

The strings G, B, and e are played open.

### Tips for Success
1. Arch your fingers so only the fingertips touch the strings
2. Keep your fingers perpendicular to the frets
3. Position your fingers close to (but not on) the fret wire
4. Check that each note rings out clearly

### Common Problems
- Finger accidentally touching adjacent strings
- Not pressing hard enough
- Fingers too far from the fret

## Exercise
1. Form the chord
2. Play each string individually
3. Make sure all notes ring clearly
4. If a string buzzes, adjust your finger position

## Challenge
Play a clean G chord, strum down once, and hold for 5 seconds without any buzzy notes!
