# Level 6: G Major

## Lesson

The **G Major chord** is one of the most important chords in guitar playing. It uses three fingers placed on specific frets of the lower strings while two strings remain open. Place your **index finger** on the 3rd fret of the low E string (the thickest string), your **middle finger** on the 2nd fret of the A string, and your **ring finger** on the 3rd fret of the D string. The high E and B strings are played open, giving the chord its bright, full sound. G Major is the foundation of countless songs across every genre from country to rock to pop. The chord shape forms a recognizable pattern you'll use frequently.

## Exercise

Practice the G Major chord for 1 minute:
1. Place your index finger on the 3rd fret of the low E string
2. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the A string
3. Place your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the D string
4. Strum all six strings
5. Check for any muted or buzzing strings
6. Adjust finger positions as needed
7. Repeat the process

Focus on clean, clear sound from every string.
