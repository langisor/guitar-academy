# Level 7: C Major

## Lesson

The **C Major chord** is a fundamental chord that uses a "stretchy" fingering pattern. Place your **index finger** on the 1st fret of the B string (the second thinnest string), your **middle finger** on the 2nd fret of the D string, and your **ring finger** on the 3rd fret of the A string. The G, high E, and low E strings are played open. A common mistake is muting the open G string with the middle finger—keep your fingers arched and fretted notes pressed firmly. C Major is incredibly common in popular music, appearing in countless songs from the Beatles to Taylor Swift. The stretch between your index and middle fingers is the primary challenge, so practice this fingering regularly.

## Exercise

Practice the C Major chord for 1 minute:
1. Place your index finger on the 1st fret of the B string
2. Place your middle finger on the 2nd fret of the D string
3. Place your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the A string
4. Strum from the A string down (skip the low E)
5. Ensure the open G string is not muted
6. Check each note rings clearly
7. Repeat the process

Focus on the stretch between your index and middle fingers.
