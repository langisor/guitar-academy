# Level 21: I-IV-V Progression

## Lesson

The **I-IV-V progression** is the most fundamental chord progression in Western music, appearing in countless songs across every genre. In the key of C major, this progression uses **C (I)**, **F (IV)**, and **G (V)**. The Roman numerals represent the scale degrees: I is the tonic (home), IV is the subdominant (departure), and V is the dominant (tension that resolves back to I). This progression creates a satisfying musical journey: stability (C), movement (F), tension (G), resolution (C). It's the backbone of blues, rock, country, and pop music. Understanding this progression gives you the key to thousands of songs and helps you understand how music moves harmonically.

## Exercise

Practice the I-IV-V progression in C major:
1. Start with C Major chord (I)
2. Count 4 beats, then transition to F Major (IV)
3. Count 4 beats, then transition to G Major (V)
4. Count 4 beats, then return to C Major (I)
5. Use the country/folk strumming pattern: D-D-U-D-U-D
6. Practice the full sequence 10 times
7. Focus on smooth transitions between each chord
8. Keep a steady, consistent rhythm
9. Try the progression in different keys (G-C-D, D-G-A)
10. Listen to how the progression feels complete and satisfying

This progression is the foundation of popular music!
