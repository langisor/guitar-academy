# Level 18: Country/Folk Pattern

## Lesson

The **country/folk pattern (D-D-U-D-U-D)** is often called the "Boom-Chicka" rhythm. It's one of the most recognizable strumming patterns in popular music. The "boom" is the down stroke on the beat, and "chicka" is the up stroke on the off-beat. This pattern creates a driving, uplifting feel essential to country, folk, and acoustic music. The key is emphasizing the downbeats (1 and 3) while keeping the upstrokes lighter. Practice slowly at first, focusing on the "boom-chicka" sound, then gradually increase speed. Once mastered, this pattern opens the door to countless acoustic songs.

## Exercise

Practice the country/folk pattern at 100 BPM:
1. Set metronome to 100 BPM
2. Strum down on beat 1 ("boom")
3. Strum down on the "and" after beat 1
4. Strum up on beat 2 ("chicka")
5. Strum down on beat 3 ("boom")
6. Strum up on beat 4 ("chicka")
7. Practice for 2 minutes continuously
8. Focus on the accent pattern: boom-CHICKA-boom-CHICKA
