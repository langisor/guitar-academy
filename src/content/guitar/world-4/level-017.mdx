# Level 17: Down-Up Pattern

## Lesson

The **down-up pattern (D-U-D-U)** is the most important strumming pattern in guitar playing. It uses 1/8th note subdivisions, giving you twice as many strums per measure as the down strum alone. The pattern alternates between downward and upward strokes, creating a continuous, flowing rhythm. Lead with your wrist—not your whole arm—and keep the motion small and controlled. The up stroke should be lighter than the down stroke. This pattern is the foundation for almost every strumming pattern you'll learn, from country to rock to folk. Master this, and you can play thousands of songs.

## Exercise

Practice the down-up pattern at 90 BPM:
1. Set metronome to 90 BPM
2. Strum down on beats 1 and 3
3. Strum up on beats 2 and 4
4. Keep your wrist loose and relaxed
5. Make the down stroke louder than up stroke
6. Maintain steady rhythm for 2 minutes
7. Gradually increase tempo

The key is continuous motion—never stop moving your hand.
