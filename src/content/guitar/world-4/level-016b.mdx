# Level 16: Reading Guitar Tablature

## Lesson

**Tablature (tab)** is a simplified notation system specifically designed for guitar that shows you exactly where to place your fingers on the fretboard. Tab consists of six lines representing the six strings—the top line is the high e string, and the bottom line is the low E string. Numbers on the lines indicate which fret to press: "0" means play the open string, "3" means press the 3rd fret, and so on. Numbers read from left to right in the order they're played. Tab is incredibly useful because it's easy to learn and shows exact finger positions, making it perfect for learning riffs, solos, and songs quickly. While tab doesn't indicate rhythm as precisely as standard notation, it's the most practical way for guitarists to share music.

## Exercise

Learn to read and play tablature with these examples:
1. The six lines represent strings: high e (top) to low E (bottom)
2. A "0" means play the open string—no fingers needed
3. A "3" means press the 3rd fret of that string
4. Numbers stacked vertically are played simultaneously (chords)
5. Numbers in sequence are played one after another
6. Practice reading: e|---0-1-2-3---| means play open, 1st, 2nd, 3rd frets on high e
7. Practice reading: e|---0---| B|---1---| G|---0---| means play a C chord shape
8. Find simple tabs online and practice reading them slowly
9. Focus on accuracy first—speed will come with practice
10. Use tab to learn your favorite riffs and songs

Tab is your gateway to learning any guitar music!
