# Level 16: Hammer-Ons and Pull-Offs

## Lesson

**Hammer-ons** and **pull-offs** are essential legato techniques that allow you to play smooth, connected notes without re-picking each one. A **hammer-on** is performed by picking a note and then forcefully "hammering" a finger onto a higher fret to sound a second note. A **pull-off** is the reverse: you pick a note and then pull your finger off to reveal a lower note that's already being fretted. These techniques are fundamental to lead guitar playing and are used extensively in solos, riffs, and melodic passages. The key to both is strength and precision—hammer-ons need a firm, decisive strike, while pull-offs require a slight downward pluck with the fretting finger as you release.

## Exercise

Practice hammer-ons and pull-offs with this routine:
1. Place your index finger on the 5th fret of the low E string
2. Pick the note, then hammer your ring finger onto the 7th fret
3. The second note should sound without re-picking
4. Practice this motion 10 times, focusing on a strong hammer
5. Now practice pull-offs: fret both 5th and 7th frets
6. Pick the 7th fret note, then pull off to the 5th fret
7. The pull-off should create a slight plucking motion
8. Practice alternating: hammer-on, pull-off, hammer-on, pull-off
9. Apply this technique to different strings and fret positions
10. Start slowly at 60 BPM, then gradually increase speed

The goal is clear, even notes—no re-picking!
