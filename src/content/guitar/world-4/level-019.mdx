# Level 19: Pop Pattern

## Lesson

The **pop strumming pattern (D-U-D-U-D-U)** creates a constant, driving rhythm essential to modern pop music. Unlike patterns with rests, this one keeps your hand in continuous motion—never stopping. The pattern gives you six strums per measure: down on beats 1 and 3, up on beats 2 and 4, with additional strums filling the spaces between. The key technique is maintaining momentum through every beat. Your hand should feel like a pendulum that never stops swinging. This pattern is perfect for upbeat pop songs and gives you a solid foundation for more complex rhythms.

## Exercise

Practice the pop pattern at 110 BPM:
1. Set metronome to 110 BPM
2. Strum down on beat 1
3. Strum up on the "and" after beat 1
4. Strum down on beat 2
5. Strum up on the "and" after beat 2
6. Strum down on beat 3
7. Strum up on the "and" after beat 3
8. Strum down on beat 4
9. Maintain continuous motion for 2 minutes
10. Never stop moving your hand
