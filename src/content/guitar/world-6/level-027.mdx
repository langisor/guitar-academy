# Level 27: B Minor Barre

## Lesson

The **B Minor barre chord** uses the **A-shape moved up** the neck, with the root on the 5th string. This is one of the trickiest barre chords because the index finger must stretch across five strings at the 2nd fret. The shape is: index finger bars the 2nd fret, middle finger on the 4th fret of the D string, ring finger on the 4th fret of the G string, and pinky on the 4th fret of the B string. The key is building enough strength in your index finger to cleanly fret all five strings while maintaining the curved shape needed for the open A string to ring. This chord is essential for countless songs in minor keys.

## Exercise

Practice the B Minor barre chord:
1. Place your index finger across the 2nd fret (strings 1-5)
2. Your index should bar strings A through high e
3. Place your middle finger on the 4th fret of the D string
4. Place your ring finger on the 4th fret of the G string
5. Place your pinky on the 4th fret of the B string
6. The low E string should remain open
7. Check that all fretted notes ring clearly
8. Practice for 2 minutes, building strength

The root note is on the 5th string (A).
