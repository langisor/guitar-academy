# Level 29: Moving the Shapes

## Lesson

Once you master the **F Major barre chord shape**, you can move it anywhere on the neck to play different chords. This is called **transposing** the shape. Move the F shape to the **3rd fret** to get **G Major** (because F + 2 frets = G), or to the **5th fret** to get **A Major**. The fingering stays exactly the same—you just slide the entire shape up or down the neck. This technique is incredibly powerful because one shape gives you access to all 12 major chords. Practice sliding smoothly between frets while maintaining consistent pressure and shape. This is the key to unlocking the entire fretboard.

## Exercise

Practice moving the F shape up the neck:
1. Start with your F Major barre chord
2. Slide the entire shape to the 3rd fret (G Major)
3. Check that all notes ring clearly
4. Slide back to F (1st fret)
5. Slide to the 5th fret (A Major)
6. Check that all notes ring clearly
7. Repeat the slide between G and A 10 times
8. Focus on smooth, controlled movement

The key is sliding smoothly without lifting fingers.
