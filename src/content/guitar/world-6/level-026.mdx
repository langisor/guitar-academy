# Level 26: F Major Barre

## Lesson

The **F Major barre chord** is the "wall" that stops many beginners—hence the name. It requires you to use your **index finger as a "bar"** across all six strings while your other fingers form the chord shape. Place your index finger flat across the 1st fret, then stack your middle finger on the 3rd fret of the A string, ring finger on the 3rd fret of the D string, and pinky on the 3rd fret of the G string. This creates the E Major shape moved to the 1st fret. The key is applying enough pressure with your index finger while keeping it curved enough to avoid muting strings. This chord opens the door to playing any major or minor chord anywhere on the neck.

## Exercise

Practice the F Major barre chord:
1. Place your index finger across the 1st fret (all 6 strings)
2. Curl your index finger to avoid muting strings
3. Place your middle finger on the 3rd fret of the A string
4. Place your ring finger on the 3rd fret of the D string
5. Place your pinky on the 3rd fret of the G string
6. Strum and check each note rings clearly
7. Practice for 2 minutes, building finger strength
8. Focus on even pressure across all strings

This chord takes time—be patient and practice daily.
