# Level 28: D Minor Barre

## Lesson

The **D Minor barre chord** uses the **D-shape moved up** the neck. This is challenging because your index finger must cover five strings (A through high e) while your other fingers form the D-shaped triad. The shape is: index bars the 5th fret, middle finger on the 7th fret of the G string, ring finger on the 7th fret of the B string, and pinky on the 7th fret of the high e string. The D and G strings should remain open. Building finger strength and learning to roll your index finger slightly toward the thumb helps achieve clean notes. This chord completes your set of essential minor barre chords.
